I photograph everyday life as it unfolds quietly — unannounced and often unnoticed. Film allows me to slow down and witness these fleeting arrangements of light, time, and presence with care.

What began as curiosity gradually became a way of paying attention. The mechanics of film — chemistry, physics, and mathematics — gift me moments that can be held and revisited. In that process of seeing and preserving, I find a quiet satisfaction and a recurring sense of joy that keeps me returning.

This archive is an ongoing body of work — a long-term observation of spaces, people, and passing moments that shape everyday life.
